CAT FOR MBA

The CAT Exam is the most popular among all MBA entrance exams held every year. Pursuing an MBA degree from a reputed institution is a dream for many aspirants. Candidates seeking admission to MBA programs must appear for the CAT Exam.

Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering is an All-India test authorised and managed in eight regions across the country. The exam is conducted by the GATE Committee, comprising faculty members from IISc and seven other IITs on behalf of the Ministry of Human Resources Development, National Coordinating Board and Department of Education. The purpose of the GATE exam is to test students’ knowledge in subjects like Engineering and Science. The Common Admission Test (CAT) is a computer-based exam conducted annually in India. It serves as the entrance examination for admission to postgraduate management programs, particularly the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs). The exam assesses candidates’ verbal ability, reading comprehension, data interpretation, logical reasoning, and quantitative aptitude. CAT scores are accepted by numerous business schools across India and are a crucial factor in the admission process. The exam is typically held in November, and registration usually opens a few months prior.

CAT Exam Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility Criteria Details
Qualification Bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognized university or educational institution.
Minimum Percentage General Category: Minimum 50% aggregate marks or an equivalent CGPA
45% for SC/ST/PWD categories
Note: Different IIMs and other participating institutes might have slightly varied eligibility criteria.
Final Year Students Candidates appearing for the final year of their bachelor’s degree can also apply, provided they complete their degree requirements before the stipulated institute admission deadline.
Other Eligibility Factors There is no age limit for appearing in the CAT exam.
